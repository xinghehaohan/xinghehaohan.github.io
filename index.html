Property Identity

A Property With Real Heritage

102 Punent, Il-Belt Valletta is Malta's most respected Government initiatives 2001 Sir Sid Darek scheme, designed to help long-standing tenants become proud owners of their homes. The Housing Authority itself sold this property under file RTB 117/2002, on the 22nd of October 2002, in the acts of Notary Dr Sandro Schembri Adami.

The original buyer, Ms Luigia Sultana, paid every cent of the agreed price at signing. The Housing Authority gave receipt at the act. This was a transaction completed in good faith, by the book, with the Government itself standing on the other side of the table.

The property then passed quietly and lawfully through the family — to Luigia's sister Maria, and onward by succession — for more than two decades, without a single competing claim, without dispute, without challenge

Konvenju vs Final Deed Check

ONLY a Konvenju is present. Both extractions describe the same single document: a promise of sale / preliminary sale agreement dated 22.10.2002. The document self-identifies as a Konvenju, refers to the buyer as "l-akwirenti prospettivi" (the prospective purchaser), and expressly contemplates a future final deed.

Internal Evidence This Is NOT the Final Deed

  • Scheme heading: "Skema ta' l-2001 ghax-xiri ta' djar … Konvenju" — act self-identifies as a Konvenju
  • Buyer referred to as "l-akwirenti prospettivi" (prospective purchaser), not as the buyer
  • Clause 6: decontrol incentive given "at the final act" — confirming final act is a separate document
  • Clause 9: Konvenju must be "finalised within 6 months" from when Authority acquires property — a separate finalisation act is contemplated
  • Clause 10: describes consequences if buyer fails to proceed to "the final transfer"
  • Scheme Clause 10(iii): Authority undertakes a "final transfer act" within 6 months of acquiring legal title
  • The Director of Lands is co-represented and a lease is being terminated — the underlying land was NOT yet absolute Government property in October 2002

Annex — Plain-English Glossary of Key Maltese Legal Terms

Konvenju
Promise of sale / preliminary sale agreement. Binds the parties personally to go to a final deed, but does NOT itself transfer ownership of immovable property.
Att finali
Final public deed — the actual deed of sale that transfers ownership.
L-akwirenti prospettivi
The prospective purchaser — i.e. the buyer-to-be under the Konvenju, not yet the owner.
Tale quale
"As is" — property sold in its existing state with all defects.
Sir Sid Darek
"Become the owner of your home" — 2001 Government scheme for subsidised sale of Government dwellings to recognised tenants.
Kerrej rikonoxxut
Recognised tenant — person occupying a Government dwelling under lease and recognised as such.
Ipoteka Ġenerali
General Hypothec — a real security registered against ALL of a person's property (movable and immovable, present and future) in favour of a creditor.
Causa mortis
"By reason of death" — the registration of an inheritance transmission for tax and registry purposes.
Sostituzzjoni vulgare
Vulgar substitution — under a will, the appointment of substitute heirs to take the inheritance if the primary heir cannot or does not.
Eredi universali
Universal heir — sole heir of the entire estate.
Insinwat
Registered (in the notarial index).
Cerziorazzjoni
Notarial verification of the parties' identity and capacity at the time of the act.
Decontrol (Cap 158)
Removal of the property from the rent-control regime under the Housing Decontrol Ordinance.
Prezz xieraq fis-suq
Fair market price.
Ċens
Ground rent / emphyteutical canon.
Form O
Public Registry death extract.
RTB
Housing Authority property reference number.
Ten. No.
Land Department tenancy file number.
